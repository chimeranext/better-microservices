# ISO 22000:2018 -- Food Safety Management Systems

**Standard:** ISO 22000:2018  
**Full title:** Food safety management systems -- Requirements for any organization in the food chain  
**Published:** June 19, 2018 (2nd edition, replaces ISO 22000:2005)  
**Technical Committee:** ISO/TC 34/SC 17, Management systems for food safety  
**Status:** Published (reviewed and confirmed in 2023)  
**Certified sites worldwide:** 51,535+ (as of 2022)

---

## 1. Scope (Clause 1)

ISO 22000:2018 specifies requirements for a food safety management system (FSMS) for organizations that need to demonstrate their ability to control food safety hazards in order to ensure that food is safe at the time of human consumption.

The standard is applicable to **all organizations in the food chain**, regardless of size or complexity, including:

- Feed and animal food producers
- Harvesters of wild plants and animals
- Farmers and ingredient producers
- Food manufacturers and processors
- Transport and storage operators
- Subcontractors and outsourced service providers
- Retail and food service outlets (caterers, restaurants)
- Suppliers of equipment, cleaning agents, packaging materials, and other food contact materials

The standard enables organizations to:
- Plan, implement, operate, maintain, and update an FSMS
- Provide products and services that are safe for the consumer
- Demonstrate compliance with applicable statutory and regulatory food safety requirements
- Demonstrate conformity with mutually agreed customer food safety requirements
- Effectively communicate food safety issues to interested parties within the food chain
- Ensure conformity with the stated food safety policy

---

## 2. Normative References (Clause 2)

There are no normative references in ISO 22000:2018. This clause is included to retain alignment with the Annex SL high-level structure numbering.

The standard does reference the **Codex Alimentarius General Principles of Food Hygiene (CXC 1-1969)**, which includes the 12 HACCP application steps.

## 3. Terms and Definitions (Clause 3)

ISO 22000:2018 defines 45 terms organized alphabetically. Key definitions include:

### 3.1 Acceptable level
Level of a food safety hazard not to be exceeded in the end product provided by the organization.

### 3.2 Action criterion
Measurable or observable specification for the monitoring of an OPRP, established to determine whether an OPRP remains in control. Distinguishes between what is acceptable (criterion met = OPRP operating as intended) and unacceptable (criterion not met = OPRP not operating as intended).

### 3.3 Control measure
Action or activity that can be used to prevent a significant food safety hazard or reduce it to an acceptable level.

### 3.4 Correction
Action to eliminate a detected nonconformity. Includes handling of potentially unsafe products and can be, for example, reprocessing, further processing, and/or elimination of the adverse consequences of the nonconformity (such as disposal for other use or specific labelling).

### 3.5 Corrective action
Action to eliminate the cause of a nonconformity and to prevent recurrence. There can be more than one cause for a nonconformity, and corrective action includes cause analysis.

### 3.6 Critical Control Point (CCP)
Step in the process at which control measure(s) is (are) applied to prevent or reduce a significant food safety hazard to an acceptable level, and defined critical limit(s) and measurement enable the application of corrections.

### 3.7 Critical limit
Measurable value which separates acceptability from unacceptability at a CCP.

### 3.8 Food safety
Assurance that food will not cause adverse health effects to the consumer when it is prepared and/or eaten according to its intended use.

### 3.9 Food safety hazard
Biological, chemical, or physical agent in food with the potential to cause an adverse health effect. Includes allergens, radiological substances, and contaminants. Covers hazards whether chronic or acute that may make food injurious to health.

### 3.10 Food safety management system (FSMS)
Management system that addresses food safety across all processes in the food chain.

### 3.11 Food safety policy
Overall intentions and direction of an organization related to food safety as formally expressed by top management.

### 3.12 Monitoring
Conducting a planned sequence of observations or measurements to assess whether a process is operating as intended. Applied **during** an activity; provides information for action within a specified time frame.

### 3.13 Operational Prerequisite Programme (OPRP)
Control measure or combination of control measures applied to prevent or reduce a significant food safety hazard to an acceptable level, and where action criterion and measurement or observation enable effective control of the process and/or product.

### 3.14 Prerequisite Programme (PRP)
Basic conditions and activities that are necessary within the organization and throughout the food chain to maintain food safety. PRPs depend on the segment of the food chain and the type of organization. Examples include Good Manufacturing Practices (GMP), Good Hygiene Practices (GHP), Good Agricultural Practices (GAP).

### 3.15 Traceability
Ability to follow the movement of a feed or food through specified stage(s) of production, processing and distribution.

### 3.16 Validation
Obtaining evidence that a control measure or combination of control measures will be capable of effectively controlling the significant food safety hazard. Applied **prior to** an activity; provides information about the capability to deliver intended results.

### 3.17 Verification
Confirmation, through the provision of objective evidence, that specified requirements have been fulfilled. Applied **after** an activity; provides information for confirmation of conformity.

## 4. Context of the Organization (Clause 4)

### 4.1 Understanding the organization and its context
Determine internal and external issues relevant to its purpose and that affect its ability to achieve the intended results of the FSMS.

### 4.2 Understanding the needs and expectations of interested parties
Identify relevant interested parties (owners, customers, employees, suppliers, regulators, auditors) and determine their requirements relevant to the FSMS.

### 4.3 Determining the scope of the FSMS
Define scope by specifying:
- Products or product categories
- Processes
- Production sites
The scope shall include all products and services that fall within the food chain.

### 4.4 Food safety management system
Establish, implement, maintain, and continually improve an FSMS including the processes needed and their interactions.

---

## 5. Leadership (Clause 5)

### 5.1 Leadership and commitment
Top management shall demonstrate leadership and commitment by:
- Ensuring the food safety policy and objectives are established and compatible with strategic direction
- Ensuring integration of FSMS requirements into business processes
- Ensuring resources are available
- Communicating the importance of effective food safety management
- Ensuring the FSMS achieves intended results
- Directing and supporting persons to contribute to FSMS effectiveness
- Promoting continual improvement
- Supporting other relevant management roles

### 5.2 Food safety policy
Top management shall establish, implement, and maintain a food safety policy that:
- Is appropriate to the purpose and context of the organization
- Provides a framework for setting FSMS objectives
- Includes a commitment to satisfy applicable food safety requirements
- Addresses internal and external communication
- Includes a commitment to continual improvement
- Ensures adequate competence for food safety

### 5.3 Organizational roles, responsibilities, and authorities
Top management shall assign responsibility and authority for:
- Ensuring the FSMS conforms to ISO 22000 requirements
- Reporting FSMS performance to top management
- Appointing the food safety team and food safety team leader
- Ensuring competence of the food safety team members

---

## 6. Planning (Clause 6)

### 6.1 Actions to address risks and opportunities
When planning for the FSMS, consider internal and external issues (4.1) and the needs of interested parties (4.2). Determine risks and opportunities that need to be addressed and plan actions to address them.

### 6.2 Objectives of the FSMS and planning to achieve them
Establish measurable food safety objectives at relevant functions, levels, and processes. Objectives shall:
- Be consistent with the food safety policy
- Be measurable (if practicable)
- Take into account applicable food safety requirements
- Be monitored and verified
- Be communicated and updated as appropriate

### 6.3 Planning of changes
Changes to the FSMS shall be carried out in a planned manner, considering:
- The purpose of the changes and their potential consequences
- The continuing integrity of the FSMS
- The availability of resources
- The allocation or reallocation of responsibilities and authorities

---

## 7. Support (Clause 7)

### 7.1 Resources
The organization shall determine and provide resources needed for the establishment, implementation, maintenance, and continual improvement of the FSMS, including:
- People
- Infrastructure
- Work environment
- Externally developed FSMS elements
- Control of externally provided processes, products, or services

### 7.2 Competence
Determine the necessary competence of persons doing work that affects food safety performance, ensure they are competent on the basis of appropriate education, training, or experience.

### 7.3 Awareness
Persons doing work under the organization's control shall be aware of the food safety policy, relevant FSMS objectives, their contribution to FSMS effectiveness, and implications of not conforming.

### 7.4 Communication
Determine internal and external communications relevant to the FSMS including what, when, with whom, how, and who communicates. Interactive communication along the food chain is mandatory.

### 7.5 Documented information
The FSMS shall include documented information required by the standard and determined by the organization as necessary for FSMS effectiveness. Control of documented information includes creation, updating, storage, and disposition.

## 8. Operation (Clause 8)

This is the most extensive and technical clause, containing the core food safety controls.

### 8.1 Operational planning and control
Plan, implement, control, maintain, and update the processes needed to realize safe products.

### 8.2 Prerequisite programmes (PRPs)
Establish, implement, maintain, and update PRP(s) to facilitate prevention and/or reduction of contaminants. PRPs shall:
- Be appropriate to the organization and its context
- Be appropriate to the size and type of operation and the nature of products
- Be implemented across the entire production system
- Be approved by the food safety team

PRP topics include (referencing ISO/TS 22002 series):
- Construction and layout of buildings and associated utilities
- Layout of premises including workspace and employee facilities
- Supplies of air, water, energy, and other utilities
- Waste and sewage management
- Equipment suitability, and its accessibility for cleaning, maintenance, and preventive maintenance
- Management of purchased materials
- Measures for prevention of cross-contamination
- Cleaning and sanitizing
- Pest control
- Personnel hygiene

### 8.3 Traceability system
The traceability system shall be able to identify incoming material from suppliers and the route of distribution of the end product. The system shall consider:
- Relation of lots/batches of materials received to the lots/batches of end products
- Materials rework
- End product distribution
Traceability records shall be retained for a defined period that as a minimum includes the shelf life of the product.

### 8.4 Emergency preparedness and response
Top management shall ensure procedures are in place for potential emergency situations or incidents that can have an impact on food safety, relevant to the organization's role in the food chain.

### 8.5 Hazard analysis
#### 8.5.1 Preliminary steps to enable hazard analysis
- Describe the characteristics of raw materials, ingredients, and product-contact materials
- Describe the characteristics of end products
- Describe the intended use
- Prepare flow diagrams for all products/product categories
- Describe processes and the process environment

#### 8.5.2 Hazard analysis
For each identified hazard, determine:
- The nature of the hazard (biological, chemical, physical)
- Its occurrence, including likelihood and severity
- Its acceptable level in the end product

#### 8.5.3 Validation of control measure(s) and combination(s) of control measures
Before implementation and after any change, validate that the control measures are capable of achieving the intended control.

#### 8.5.4 Hazard control plan (HACCP/OPRP plan)
For each CCP and OPRP, document:
- Food safety hazard(s) to be controlled
- Control measure(s)
- Critical limit(s) (CCP) or action criteria (OPRP)
- Monitoring procedures
- Corrections and corrective actions
- Responsibilities and authorities
- Monitoring records

### 8.6 Updating preliminary information and documents specifying PRPs and the hazard control plan

### 8.7 Control of monitoring and measuring
Ensure monitoring and measuring equipment is calibrated or verified at specified intervals.

### 8.8 Verification related to PRPs and the hazard control plan
Establish, implement, and maintain verification activities.

### 8.9 Control of product and process nonconformities
#### 8.9.1 General
#### 8.9.2 Corrections
#### 8.9.3 Corrective actions
#### 8.9.4 Handling of potentially unsafe products
#### 8.9.5 Withdrawal/recall

## 9. Performance Evaluation (Clause 9)

### 9.1 Monitoring, measurement, analysis, and evaluation
Determine what needs to be monitored and measured, the methods, when monitoring shall be performed, when results shall be analyzed, and who shall analyze them.

### 9.2 Internal audit
Conduct internal audits at planned intervals to provide information on whether the FSMS conforms to planned arrangements and ISO 22000 requirements and is effectively implemented and maintained.

### 9.3 Management review
Top management shall review the FSMS at planned intervals to ensure its continuing suitability, adequacy, and effectiveness. Input to management review shall include:
- Status of actions from previous management reviews
- Changes in internal and external issues relevant to the FSMS
- Information on FSMS performance and effectiveness
- Adequacy of resources
- Results of system updating activities
- Effectiveness of actions taken to address risks and opportunities

---

## 10. Improvement (Clause 10)

### 10.1 Nonconformity and corrective action
When a nonconformity occurs, react to it, evaluate the need for action to eliminate causes, implement needed action, review effectiveness, and make changes to the FSMS if necessary.

### 10.2 Continual improvement
Continually improve the suitability, adequacy, and effectiveness of the FSMS through the use of communication, management review, internal audit, analysis of results of verification activities, validation of control measures, corrective actions, and FSMS updating.

### 10.3 Update of the FSMS
Top management shall ensure the FSMS is continually updated. The food safety team shall evaluate the FSMS at planned intervals and then consider whether it is necessary to review the hazard analysis, the hazard control plan, and established PRPs.

## Key Conceptual Framework

### PDCA Cycle (Two Levels)
ISO 22000:2018 operates two nested Plan-Do-Check-Act cycles:

**Organizational PDCA** (Clauses 4-10): Manages the overall FSMS strategy, resources, and oversight.

**Operational PDCA** (Clause 8): Manages PRPs, OPRPs, and CCPs within the hazard control plan.

### Three Pillars of ISO 22000
1. **Interactive communication** -- Clear, timely food safety information exchange across the supply chain
2. **System management** -- Integration of food safety into overall business processes
3. **HACCP principles** -- Preventive hazard identification, evaluation, and control

### Control Measure Hierarchy
- **PRP** -- Foundation-level: basic hygiene and operational conditions
- **OPRP** -- Mid-level: specific operational controls for significant hazards, with action criteria
- **CCP** -- Top-level: process steps with critical limits where hazards can be prevented or eliminated

## Relationship to Other Standards

- **ISO/TS 22002 series** -- Prerequisite programs (6 parts: manufacturing, catering, farming, packaging, transport/storage, feed/animal food)
- **ISO/TS 22003** -- Requirements for audit and certification bodies
- **ISO/TS 22004** -- Application guidance
- **ISO 22005** -- Traceability system design
- **ISO 9001** -- Quality management systems (shared Annex SL structure)
- **ISO 14001** -- Environmental management systems (shared Annex SL structure)
- **ISO 45001** -- Occupational health and safety (shared Annex SL structure)
- **FSSC 22000** -- Food Safety System Certification (builds on ISO 22000)
- **Codex Alimentarius CXC 1-1969** -- General Principles of Food Hygiene (HACCP)

## Regulatory Recognition

- **European Union:** Recognized as voluntary compliance tool for EU Regulation 852/2004
- **United States:** Supports FSMA Final Rule for Preventive Controls for Human Food
- **Developing economies:** India and Brazil increasingly adopt it for global market access
- **International endorsement:** WHO and WTO recommend harmonizing requirements around international standards

# ISO 28000:2022 -- Security and Resilience -- Security Management Systems

**Standard:** ISO 28000:2022  
**Full title:** Security and resilience -- Security management systems -- Requirements  
**Published:** March 15, 2022 (2nd edition, replaces ISO 28000:2007)  
**Technical Committee:** ISO/TC 292, Security and resilience  
**Status:** Published  
**Structure:** Annex SL high-level structure (10 clauses)  
**Certified organizations worldwide:** 5,000+

---

## 1. Scope (Clause 1)

ISO 28000:2022 specifies requirements for a security management system, including those aspects critical to the security assurance of the supply chain. The requirements are generic and intended to be applicable to all organizations, regardless of type, size, and industry. The extent of applicability depends on the organization's operating environment and complexity.

The standard provides a holistic and common approach that is not industry or sector specific. It can be applied to any activity, internal or external, at all levels, and throughout an organization's lifespan.

**Key change from 2007:** The original ISO 28000:2007 focused narrowly on supply chain logistics security (post-9/11 context). The 2022 revision represents a fundamental transformation, expanding scope from supply-chain-specific to comprehensive organizational security management while maintaining full supply chain applicability.

## 3. Terms and Definitions (Clause 3)

### 3.1 Security management system (SeMS)
A structured system of coordinated policies, procedures, processes, practices, and controls through which an organization manages its security objectives.

### 3.2 Security risk
The potential for loss, damage, or disruption due to security threats, including theft, terrorism, cyberattacks, natural events, sabotage, or other hostile actions.

### 3.3 Security threat
[Content not publicly available -- requires ISO purchase]

Key terminology in Clause 3 is known to include: threat, vulnerability, asset, incident, interested parties, preventive action, and mitigation measures.

### 3.4 Interested parties
Internal and external stakeholders with security-relevant requirements, including employees, regulators, clients, partners, and the public.

### 3.5 Preventive action
Action eliminating causes of potential security incidents before they occur.

### 3.6 Mitigation measures
Controls reducing the likelihood or impact of security threats.

## 4. Context of the Organization (Clause 4)

### 4.1 Understanding the organization and its context
Determine internal and external issues relevant to the organization's purpose and strategic direction that affect its ability to achieve the intended outcomes of the security management system.

Considerations include:
- **Political factors:** Political stability, government policy, regulatory requirements
- **Technological factors:** Technological change, cybersecurity landscape
- **Economic factors:** Economic conditions, market volatility
- **Social factors:** Social and cultural environment, workforce dynamics
- **Environmental factors:** Natural hazards, climate-related risks
- **Legal factors:** Applicable legislation, contractual obligations

### 4.2 Understanding the needs and expectations of interested parties
Identify internal and external stakeholders and determine their security-related requirements.

### 4.3 Determining the scope of the security management system
Define the scope across organizational activities, products, services, and locations. Consider supply chain dependencies and interdependencies.

### 4.4 Security management system
Establish, implement, maintain, and continually improve the security management system, including the processes needed and their interactions.

> **Note:** Clause 4 includes recommendations on principles for better coordination with ISO 31000 (Risk management).

## 5. Leadership (Clause 5)

### 5.1 Leadership and commitment
Top management shall demonstrate leadership and commitment by:
- Establishing a security policy aligned with organizational purpose and strategy
- Integrating security requirements into business processes
- Ensuring resource availability (personnel, training, budget, technology, infrastructure)
- Communicating the importance of security management organization-wide
- Promoting a security culture where personnel at all levels recognize security as everyone's responsibility
- Supporting continual improvement
- Supporting other management roles in demonstrating leadership

### 5.2 Security policy
Establish and maintain a security policy that:
- Is appropriate to the purpose of the organization
- Provides a framework for setting security objectives
- Includes a commitment to satisfy applicable requirements
- Includes a commitment to continual improvement

### 5.3 Organizational roles, responsibilities, and authorities
Assign and communicate responsibilities and authorities for relevant roles within the security management system.

## 6. Planning (Clause 6)

### 6.1 Actions to address risks and opportunities

#### 6.1.1 Security risk assessment
Conduct systematic threat identification across multiple categories:

- **Physical threats:** Theft, vandalism, sabotage, terrorism, unauthorized access, trespassing
- **Personnel threats:** Insider threats, workplace violence, kidnapping, social engineering, impersonation
- **Information security threats:** Cyber-attacks, data breaches, intellectual property theft, espionage
- **Supply chain threats:** Counterfeit materials, cargo theft, contamination, tampering, supplier failures
- **Natural disaster threats:** Earthquakes, floods, hurricanes, pandemics, extreme weather
- **Geopolitical threats:** Political instability, armed conflict, sanctions, trade restrictions, embargoes

#### 6.1.2 Risk assessment methodology
Apply methodologies ranging from:
- **Qualitative:** High/medium/low risk ratings
- **Semi-quantitative:** Scoring matrices
- **Quantitative:** Probabilistic analysis with financial impact modeling

#### 6.1.3 Risk treatment
Determine risk treatment options:
- **Avoid:** Eliminate the activity that gives rise to the risk
- **Reduce:** Apply controls to reduce likelihood and/or impact
- **Transfer:** Share the risk (insurance, outsourcing, contractual arrangements)
- **Accept:** Acknowledge and monitor the risk within appetite

### 6.2 Security objectives and planning to achieve them
Establish security objectives at relevant functions, levels, and processes. Objectives shall be measurable, monitored, communicated, and updated as appropriate.

### 6.3 Planning of changes
Changes to the security management system shall be carried out in a planned manner.

## 7. Support (Clause 7)

### 7.1 Resources
Determine and provide resources for the establishment, implementation, maintenance, and continual improvement of the SMS. Resources include personnel, training, budget, technology, and infrastructure.

### 7.2 Competence
Ensure persons doing security-relevant work are competent through education, training, and experience.

### 7.3 Awareness
Persons working under the organization's control shall be aware of the security policy, their contribution, and implications of not conforming.

### 7.4 Communication
Determine internal and external communications relevant to the security management system.

### 7.5 Documented information
Maintain documented information required by the standard and determined by the organization as necessary for SMS effectiveness.

---

## 8. Operation (Clause 8)

### 8.1 Operational planning and control
Establish criteria for operational processes, implement controls ensuring proper operation, control process changes to prevent security degradation, and manage outsourced security-relevant processes.

> **Note:** Clause 8 includes recommendations for better consistency with ISO 22301, facilitating integration of security strategies, procedures, processes, treatments, and security plans.

### 8.2 Security controls implementation

#### Physical security
- Perimeter security (fencing, barriers, gates)
- Access controls (badges, biometrics, key cards)
- Surveillance systems (CCTV, monitoring)
- Lighting and visibility
- Intrusion detection systems
- Vehicle barriers and checkpoints

#### Personnel security
- Background screening and vetting
- Security clearances
- Training and awareness programs
- Visitor management procedures
- Segregation of duties
- Insider threat mitigation

#### Information security
- Access controls and authentication
- Encryption and secure communications
- Data loss prevention
- Cyber defense measures
- Network monitoring
- Secure data storage and disposal

#### Cargo security
- Container seals and tamper-evident packaging
- Inspection and verification procedures
- Secure storage facilities
- Chain of custody documentation
- Weight verification
- Non-intrusive inspection technology

#### Facility security
- Security zones and access tiers
- Vehicle barriers and blast protection
- Package and mail screening
- Security patrols (manned and electronic)
- Alarm systems
- Secure areas designation

#### Transportation security
- Road transport security protocols
- Rail security measures
- Maritime security (ISPS Code alignment)
- Air cargo security
- Intermodal transfer security

### 8.3 Emergency preparedness and response
Establish and maintain procedures for emergency situations and security incidents, including:
- Emergency response plans
- Business continuity planning
- Crisis management procedures
- Communication protocols during incidents
- Recovery procedures

## 9. Performance Evaluation (Clause 9)

### 9.1 Monitoring, measurement, analysis, and evaluation
Monitor and measure performance through security metrics including:
- Incident frequencies and severities
- Response times
- Control effectiveness ratings
- Training completion rates
- Audit findings and closure rates
- Stakeholder satisfaction
- Near-miss reporting rates

### 9.2 Internal audit
Conduct internal audits at planned intervals evaluating conformity to the organization's own requirements and ISO 28000 requirements, and effectiveness of the SMS.

### 9.3 Management review
Top management shall review the SMS at planned intervals to assess:
- Adequacy of the security management system
- Suitability for achieving security objectives
- Strategic alignment with organizational goals
- Need for changes or improvements

---

## 10. Improvement (Clause 10)

### 10.1 Nonconformity and corrective action
When a nonconformity occurs:
- React to the nonconformity, take action to control and correct it
- Evaluate the need for action to eliminate the cause(s)
- Implement any action needed
- Review the effectiveness of corrective action taken
- Make changes to the SMS if necessary

### 10.2 Continual improvement
Continually improve the suitability, adequacy, and effectiveness of the SMS through:
- Incident root cause analysis
- Proactive security enhancements
- Trend analysis
- Benchmarking against best practices
- Technology updates

## Required Documentation

Organizations implementing ISO 28000:2022 shall maintain:

- Security Management Policy
- Risk Register and Threat Assessments
- Statement of Applicability (SoA)
- Security Objectives and Key Performance Indicators
- Interested Parties and Requirements List
- Legal and Regulatory Compliance Matrix
- Roles and Responsibilities Matrix
- Emergency Response and Incident Handling Plans
- Internal Audit Program and Reports
- Corrective Action and Continual Improvement Logs
- Training and Competency Records

## Certification Process

- **Stage 1 audit:** Documentation review and readiness assessment
- **Stage 2 audit:** Comprehensive on-site implementation and effectiveness evaluation
- **Surveillance audits:** Annual verification of continued conformity
- **Recertification audit:** Every 3 years
- **Issuing bodies:** Only accredited certification bodies complying with ISO/IEC 17021-1
